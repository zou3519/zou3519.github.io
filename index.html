Projects

Todolists: A Study of Top-Down Skiplists

Ordered dictionaries that perform insert, search, and delete operations in log(n) time are vital to the performance of many computing applications.  We analyzed a recent 2014 paper by Barba and Morin proposing a new data structure, the top-down skiplist (todolist)and performed an implementation-level study.  We benchmarked the top-down skiplist against popular, industry-standard data structures such as the red-black tree and confirmed Barba and Morin's result that while todolists can be faster in terms of search time, they leave much to be desired in insertion and deletion time. Coded in Go.  Final project for Harvard's CS 223: Probablistic Algorithms and Analysis.

 

Machine Learning: Learning to Play Connect Four

A simple command line Connect Four game. We trained a neural network to play the game using genetic algorithms and a minimax AI.  Final project for Harvard's CS 51: Intro to Computer Science II. Coded in Java.
